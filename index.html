What is Child Trafficking?

Child trafficking is the illegal trade of children for various forms of exploitation, including forced labor, sexual exploitation, illegal adoption, and use in criminal activities. It is a severe violation of human rights and affects millions of children worldwide.

Child trafficking occurs through abduction, coercion, deceit, or manipulation. Victims are often subjected to horrific conditions and denied basic rights, including safety, education, and healthcare.

Statistics

Child trafficking is a global crisis, with millions of children being affected each year. Here are some shocking statistics:

  • It is estimated that 1 million children are trafficked every year worldwide.
  • Nearly 50% of trafficking victims are minors, with girls being particularly vulnerable to sexual exploitation.
  • The International Labour Organization (ILO) estimates that more than 4.3 million children are in forced labor globally.
  • Approximately 100,000 children are trafficked for sexual exploitation in the United States alone each year.
  • Victims of child trafficking are often sold for as little as $200 or less in some regions.

These statistics highlight the urgent need for global efforts to prevent trafficking and protect vulnerable children from exploitation.

  • National Human Trafficking Hotline (USA)
  • Polaris Project
  • International Organization for Migration (IOM)
  • Stop The Traffik

How You Can Help

There are many ways you can contribute to ending child trafficking:

  • Educate yourself and others about the signs of trafficking and how to spot potential victims.
  • Support organizations working to prevent trafficking and help victims recover.
  • Report suspicious activities to the authorities or the human trafficking hotline.
  • Raise awareness by sharing information on social media and encouraging others to take action.
  • Advocate for stronger laws and policies that protect children and hold traffickers accountable.
